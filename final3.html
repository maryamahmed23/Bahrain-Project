Food

Food is an important aspect in every culture. Food in Bahrain is very rich and flavorful. Any person would definitely enjoy their time eating those delicious and marvelous dishes.

Breakfast

The most famous breakfast dish is balaleet. It is vermicelli with an omlette. The mixture might gross some people out, however you could not find a more perfect combination to satisfy your taste buds. Moving along to the next dish, which is tomatoes and eggs. This dish is very self explanatory. To summarize, it is tomatoes mixed in with different spices scrambles with some eggs. The least breakfast dish is Mahyawa. This dish is like no other. If you thought balaleet did not sound appetiting, wait until you hear what mahyawa is made of. Mahyawa is basically fish oil. The way to eat it is fairly simple, grad a piece of bread or khubz and just dip away. Even though it might not sound tasty, this dish is beloved by many Bahrainis and you never know until you tried it yourself. Finally, it is not a traditional breakfast without some milk tea. It is a necessity in the Bahraini breakfast. These are only some of the traditional Bahraini breakfast dishes.

Lunch

Lunch is very simple and to the point. It is mainly rice and meat. However, the rice does come in different variations. The first dish is rice and fish. Typically, white rice is served with fish, however another rice that is known to be served with fish in Bahrain is Muhamar . It is a sweet rice that pleasures your taste buds. Another lunch dish is chicken or lamb ghoozi. This type of dish is mainly eaten during big celebrations like a wedding or eid.

Dinner

The most famous thing you can get for dinner is tikka. It can either be chicken or lamb, depending on your liking. It is basically pieces of chicken/lamb put on a skewer and grilled. To emphasize it more, you could add some yoghurt, onions or even hummus. Nothing says bahraini food like digging into some tikka in the car with some friends or family.

Dessert

Halwa is a very famous bahraini sweet dish that is loved by many. Even people from neighboring countries make sure to leave back with Halwa for their families. It contains sugar, starch, water, rose water, oil, saffron, food coloring and some nuts for garnish on top. However, it does contain alot of sugars and starch, which is a negative for some people. Another dessert that is popular in Bahrain is Aseeda. It contains many ingredients similar to Halwa. It consists of flour, water, sugar, oil, some spices and some scrambled eggs on top to compliment the flour based part of the dish.
